About Me

Project assistant at CSIR-National Chemical Laboratory in Dr.Kavita Joshi's Lab where we are developing high entropy alloys for hydrogen storage at room temperature.

Research Interests

Currently, I'm working as a Project Assistant at CSIR-National Chemical Laboratory, where I use Natural Language Processing and Large Language Models to create databases for training hydrogen storage models. Simultaneously, I am researching on hydrogen storage, investigating advanced methods for storing and utilizing hydrogen as an energy carrier, with a keen eye on addressing challenges related to efficiency and safety.

In my Master's thesis at Central University of Punjab, I studied the energy partionining of molecular hydrogen formation on interstellar dust grains using Quantum Dynamics. During my bachelor's, using a small radio telescope I did a HI-line scan of our Milky Way Galaxy and was able to determine the structure and the mass distribution of our galaxy. Blog
